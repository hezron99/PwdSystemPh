About Us

Access for All is a community-driven project that aims to improve accessibility for people with disabilities in public spaces. The project is led by a team of students and community members in AMA, who are passionate about creating inclusive environments for everyone.

Register Now!

The project focuses on three main areas: transportation, buildings, and public spaces. The team works closely with city officials, property owners, and community organizations to identify areas where accessibility can be improved. They then develop and implement solutions to make those spaces more accessible for people with disabilities..

Our team also includes several advisors and community members with disabilities, who provide valuable insights and perspectives on accessibility issues. We believe that everyone has the right to access and participate in their community, regardless of their abilities. That's why we're committed to improving accessibility in public spaces, including transportation, buildings, and public spaces..

One of the project's key initiatives is to improve accessibility in public transportation. This includes retrofitting buses and trains with accessibility features like ramps and designated seating for people with mobility impairments. The team also works to improve accessibility at bus and train stations, including installing accessible restrooms and elevators.

In addition to transportation, the project also works to make buildings and public spaces more accessible. This includes installing ramps, elevators, and automatic doors, as well as creating designated parking spaces for people with disabilities. The team also works with property owners to make sure that buildings are in compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and other accessibility standards.
